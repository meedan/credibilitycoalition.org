News credibility is the challenge of our time.

Can we agree on scientific and systematic ways to assess reliable information, and whether they can be applied at scale?

Our community-driven approach is trying to find out through collaboratively structured definitions and shared results from content tests for credibility.

RECENT MENTIONS

  • “As many independent projects are now working on fighting misinformation and analysing trust, a common framework is needed.”

  • “...to see if there can be a programmatic way for social networks use their scores to influence algorithmic rankings..”

  • “The Credibility Coalition is a diverse grassroots group of volunteers whose goals are to identify a set of credibility indicators and use cases; build cooperation around a data model; and generate and use testing data to improve the model.”

  • "Rather than build one stable definition that can be used to identify content, feature- or identifier-based approaches [like the Credibilty Coalition] work from the ground up, using methods drawn from content analysis or social network analysis to identify potential features associated with 'fake news,' disinformation, and spam."
